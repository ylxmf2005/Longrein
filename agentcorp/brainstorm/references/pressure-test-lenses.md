# Pressure-Test Lenses

Use only the lenses that fit the current ambiguity. Do not dump this list on the sponsor.

- **Problem reality** — What observable pain, workaround, cost, or demand proves this matters?
- **Specific beneficiary** — Who benefits first, and what job becomes easier?
- **Counterfactual** — What happens if nothing changes?
- **Smallest valuable version** — What is the smallest thing that still solves a real problem?
- **Attachment check** — Are we attached to a solution shape instead of the outcome?
- **Scope boundary** — What tempting adjacent work is out of scope?
- **Failure mode** — Why could this fail even if implemented well?
- **Tradeoff honesty** — What gets worse when this gets better?
- **Durability** — What likely change in product, policy, infrastructure, or usage could make this weaker?

Ask one probe at a time. If the sponsor cannot answer, record the uncertainty as an assumption or open question instead of silently resolving it.
